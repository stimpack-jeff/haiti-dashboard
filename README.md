## Key Insights

### 1. Haiti Must Build, Not Rebuild
No historical golden age to restore. Even "stable" periods (2006-2010) required 13,000 international troops and lasted only 4 years before the 2010 earthquake. **Aiming for something Haiti has never achieved: self-sustaining democratic stability.**

### 2. Gangs Are Now Autonomous
Post-2023 shift: Viv Ansanm coalition generates $60-75M/year from extortion (container tolls from DR, road checkpoints, business shakedowns). Less dependent on elite patrons than before. Controls 80-90% of Port-au-Prince.

### 3. No Single Solution Works
- **El Salvador:** 80% homicide drop via mass incarceration, but ~50% wrongful detention, 261 prison deaths
- **Medell√≠n:** 90% reduction over 25 years via social urbanism, but required baseline security first + $2-3B
- **Liberia:** DDR disarmed 100,000+ ex-combatants, but only AFTER war ended (gangs defeated first)
- **Jamaica:** Failure case - didn't break elite-gang nexus, violence persists

### 4. 20-Year Commitment Required
Medell√≠n: 25 years. Rwanda: 15+ years. Haiti never sustained 30+ years of stability. **Success probability: 40-50% IF phased strategy executed with sustained international commitment. Otherwise: 90% probability of state failure.**

## Recommended Strategy

### Phase 1: Security (Years 1-2) - $3-4B
- Large-scale military intervention (15-20k troops, not current 3k MSS)
- Targeted strikes on gang leaders
- Financial disruption (freeze elite assets)
- Border control (cut DR revenue)
- Success probability: 55%

### Phase 2: Transition (Years 2-4) - $1-2B
- DDR program (disarm 15-20k gang members)
- Security sector reform (rebuild police)
- Community-based security pilots
- Economic quick wins
- Success probability: 65% (if Phase 1 succeeded)

### Phase 3: Reconstruction (Years 5-10) - $3-5B
- Social urbanism (Medell√≠n model: cable cars, libraries, parks)
- Economic development (massive job creation)
- Institutional reform (judiciary, anti-corruption)
- Success probability: 60% (if Phases 1-2 succeeded)

### Phase 4: Sustainability (Years 10-20) - $1-2B
- Security handover to Haitian forces
- Economic self-sufficiency
- Democratic consolidation
- Success probability: 50% (if all prior phases succeeded)

**Total Cost:** $8-13 billion over 20 years  
**Comparable to:** Liberia UNMIL (~$10B) - SUCCESS | MINUSTAH ($7B) - FAILED
