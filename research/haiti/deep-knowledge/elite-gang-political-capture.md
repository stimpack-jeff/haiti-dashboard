## II. GANG-ELITE FINANCIAL FLOWS: MONEY LAUNDERING MECHANISMS

### A. How Gang Money Becomes "Clean"

**Gang Revenue (Baseline):** $60-75M/year from extortion (ICG Dec 2025)

**Money Laundering Methods (UNODC Jan 2026 Report):**

1. **Bulk Cash Smuggling:**
   - Physical transport of cash across DR border
   - Gangs control border crossings (400 Mawozo controls customs at Ganthier)
   - Cash deposited in DR banks, then wired to international accounts

2. **Unregulated Money Transfer Services:**
   - Informal remittance networks (outside formal banking)
   - Mix gang proceeds with legitimate diaspora remittances
   - Very difficult to trace (cash-based, no records)

3. **Front Companies:**
   - **"Politically-connected economic elites"** own legitimate businesses (UNODC wording)
   - Gang cash invested in construction, real estate, retail
   - Businesses report inflated revenues (gang cash = "sales")
   - **Construction materials perfect for laundering** (high cash flow, hard to verify quantities)

4. **"Smurfing":**
   - Breaking large sums into small deposits (<$10K) to avoid reporting thresholds
   - Multiple bank accounts, multiple individuals making deposits
   - Over time, large sums "cleaned" without triggering alerts

5. **Real Estate Purchases:**
   - Buy property with cash, sell later for "clean" money
   - Port-au-Prince real estate market = limited oversight
   - Property titles often disputed/unclear = easy to manipulate

6. **False Invoicing:**
   - Over-invoice imports (report higher costs than actual)
   - Under-invoice exports (report lower revenues than actual)
   - Difference = laundered money moving across borders

**Which Elite Businesses Likely Involved:**
- **Construction companies** (Bigio's GB Group - steel, cement, materials)
- **Import-export firms** (multiple families - easy to inflate/deflate invoices)
- **Retail/wholesale** (Boulos' operations, others - cash-heavy businesses)
- **Real estate development** (all major families own property portfolios)

**Evidence Level:** üü° **Moderate** (UNODC documents methods, ICG confirms elite-gang nexus, but NO SPECIFIC COMPANY NAMES PUBLICLY DISCLOSED)

**Why Elites Participate:**
1. **Profit:** Gangs pay fees for laundering services (10-20% typical in criminal networks)
2. **Protection:** Laundering gang money = gangs protect elite businesses from attacks
3. **Shared interest in state failure:** Elites maintain monopolies, gangs maintain territorial control
4. **Political leverage:** Gangs pressure government on elite behalf (or vice versa)

**Source Quality:** Tier 1 (UNODC), Tier 1-2 (ICG)  
**Confidence:** 85% on methods, 70% on elite participation pattern, 30% on WHICH specific elites/companies

### B. Elite Incentives for Blocking Reform

**Question:** WHY would Haitian elites want state failure?

   - Haiti has minimal tax collection (10-15% of GDP vs. 25-35% in functional states)
   - Elites pay virtually nothing in taxes
   - Functional state = tax enforcement = billions in lost wealth

3. **Aid Capture:**
   - International aid flows through elite-controlled businesses (contracts for supplies, logistics, construction)
   - $13B spent on MINUSTAH (2004-2017) - elites captured significant portion
   - More aid during crisis = more profit opportunities

4. **Import Control:**
   - Haiti produces almost nothing domestically (except some agriculture)
   - Elites control imports of EVERYTHING (food, fuel, consumer goods)
   - Functional state = domestic industry = competition for imports = lower profits

5. **Cheap Labor:**
   - Gang violence keeps population desperate

   - Functional state = labor protections = higher wages = lower profits

6. **Real Estate Speculation:**
   - Displacement from gang violence = people sell property cheap

   - When/if stability returns = massive appreciation

7. **Smuggling:**
   - Porous borders = untaxed, unregulated trade with DR
   - DR exports to Haiti worth billions - elites control smuggling networks
