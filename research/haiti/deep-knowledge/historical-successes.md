## What We Can Learn from These Periods

### Common Pattern: External Support + Relative Calm
All identified periods share:
1. **External security presence** (US occupation, MINUSTAH) OR strongman suppression
2. **No major external shocks** (earthquakes, hurricanes during stability)
3. **Limited duration** (longest: 22 years post-US, but unstable; most: 4-13 years)

### Critical Finding: Haiti Has NEVER Had Long-Term Self-Sustaining Stability
- No 30+ year period of functioning democratic governance
- Stability always relied on external force OR authoritarian control
- External shocks (earthquakes, hurricanes, coups) repeatedly derail progress

### Implication for Current Crisis:
**Haiti lacks historical precedent for the kind of stable, democratic state being proposed.**

This doesn't mean it's impossible, but means:
- Can't simply "restore" something that worked before (it never did long-term)
- Must BUILD something new, not rebuild something old
- 20-year commitment is realistic (longer than any historical success period)

## Comparison: Why Did Dominican Republic Succeed While Haiti Failed?

**Same island, radically different outcomes. Why?**

### Factors Favoring DR (Preliminary Hypotheses):

1. **Colonial Legacy:**
   - DR: Spanish colony (integrated into Spanish empire, institutions)
   - Haiti: French slave colony (extractive, no institution-building)

2. **Independence Process:**
   - DR: Peaceful independence from Spain (1821), brief Haiti occupation (1822-1844), then self-rule
   - Haiti: Violent revolution (1791-1804), international isolation, debt to France

3. **Economic Structure:**
   - DR: Diversified (agriculture, tourism, manufacturing, remittances)
   - Haiti: Remittance-dependent, little industry, 98% deforestation

4. **Political Culture:**
   - DR: Authoritarian but stable periods (Trujillo 1930-1961, Balaguer 1966-1978, 1986-1996)
   - Haiti: Coup-rebellion cycles, weak institutions

5. **External Relations:**
   - DR: Better US relations, tourism investment, MLB pipeline (soft power)
   - Haiti: Isolation, negative perceptions, less investment

6. **Geography:**
   - DR: Less deforestation (40% vs. Haiti's 2%), better agricultural potential
   - Haiti: Mountainous western side, more erosion, less arable land

## Case Study: RenÃ© PrÃ©val Administration (2006-2011)

**Most Recent "Success" Period - Worth Deeper Analysis**

### Context:
- Elected 2006 after Aristide's ouster (2004)
- MINUSTAH peacekeeping force providing security
- Post-conflict reconstruction phase
- Second term for PrÃ©val (first term 1996-2001, also relatively stable)

### What Worked:
1. **Political legitimacy** - Democratic election, broad support
2. **International backing** - US, UN, Caribbean support
3. **Economic growth** - 2-3% GDP growth pre-earthquake
4. **Security improvements** - Gang violence declined (but didn't disappear)
5. **Institutional rebuilding** - Police training, judicial reforms (slow progress)

### What Failed:
- **Poverty reduction minimal** - 70-80% still in poverty
- **Corruption persisted** - Elite capture continued
- **Infrastructure weak** - Vulnerable to earthquake
- **Gang dismantling incomplete** - Gangs suppressed, not eliminated
- **Dependence on external support** - MINUSTAH holding security, not Haitian forces

### 2010 Earthquake Impact:
- **220,000 dead**
- **1.5 million displaced**
- **Government buildings destroyed** (including National Palace)
- **Economy devastated** (~120% of GDP in damages)
- **Cholera outbreak** (UN peacekeepers) killed 10,000, delegitimized MINUSTAH

### Why This Period Matters:
**Closest thing to "working" Haiti in recent memory.**  
If we could restore 2006-2010 conditions + prevent earthquake/cholera, that's a realistic benchmark.

But note: Even this required 13,000 international troops and wasn't solving root causes (poverty, elite capture).
